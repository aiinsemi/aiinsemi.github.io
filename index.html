Workshop on Use of AI in Seminconductor Manufacturing


Hosted by:
Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar


6th & 7th February 2026

The Future of Semiconductor Manufacturing

The semiconductor industry is entering an era where traditional process control and optimization methods are no longer sufficient to meet the demands of advanced CMOS nodes, heterogeneous integration and complex manufacturing workflows. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) are rapidly emerging as transformative tools to enhance yield, improve process efficiency, enable predictive maintenance and accelerate technology development.

About the Workshop

As the semiconductor industry advances toward sub-2 nm CMOS nodes, heterogeneous integration, and increasingly complex high-volume manufacturing, traditional process control and optimization techniques are reaching their limits. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) are rapidly reshaping this landscapeâ€”enabling smarter fabs, predictive process control, accelerated technology development, and unprecedented improvements in yield and equipment efficiency.
This two-day workshop brings together experts from industry, academia, and government to examine how AI/ML is transforming semiconductor manufacturing. The program will feature invited talks, panel discussions, and interactive sessions focused on practical deployment of AI in fabs, emerging research directions, and the future workforce needed to drive this technological shift.

When

6th & 7th February 2026

Where

Department of Electrical Engineering
IIT Gandhinagar

Objectives

  • Introduce participants to the key concepts and applications of AI/ML in semiconductor process control, equipment monitoring, and yield improvement.
  • Highlight real-world use cases of AI in lithography optimization, defect inspection, predictive maintenance and design-to-manufacturing co-optimization.
  • Provide an understanding of AI tools, data pipelines, and algorithmic approaches relevant to semiconductor fabs.
  • Facilitate interactions between academia, industry, and government to identify research, workforce development, and technology adoption opportunities.

Target Audience

  • Semiconductor industry professionals (RnD, process engineers and tool vendors)
  • Faculty and students from IITs, NITs, and engineering colleges
  • Start-ups and researchers working in AI/ML and semiconductor manufacturing

Discussion Topics

  • AI in Semiconductor Manufacturing
  • Machine Learning for Process Optimization and Yield Enhancement
  • Predictive Maintenance and Digital Twins
  • AI for Defect Detection and Inspection
  • AI for Device and Material Modeling
  • TCAD and AI for Semiconductor Manufacturing
  • Building AI-ready talent for semiconductor manufacturing
  • Emerging research opportunities and policy directions

Student Poster Presentation

A dedicated student poster session will be organized as part of the workshop to showcase emerging ideas at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence (AI) / Machine Learning (ML) and semiconductor manufacturing. Undergraduate, postgraduate, and PhD students are invited to present posters on topics such as AI-driven process control, yield enhancement, defect inspection, equipment monitoring, digital twins, and design-to-manufacturing co-optimization.
  • Open to students from IITs, NITs, and other engineering institutes.
  • Posters may present completed work, ongoing projects, or well-motivated concepts related to AI in semiconductor manufacturing.
  • Accepted posters will be displayed in a dedicated session with opportunities to interact with industry and academic experts.
  • A Best Poster Award will be presented based on technical quality, originality, and clarity of presentation.
